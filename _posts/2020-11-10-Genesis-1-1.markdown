Genesis 1:1
In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth.

I imagine one could write an entire dissertation on this single verse alone. The book of Genesis is the introductory book of the Bible, so it is fitting for the beginning to be the beginning as the name describes. So, we are at the beginning, and in the beginning, there is God. That is, God exists.

How did he get there? There are many questions as to the nature of God, and here we see that He is present.

"He created the heavens and the earth." So there is God, the heavens, and the earth. That is, supposedly, everything in existence so far.

Now, the heavens and the earth do not consist of everything for there is God. We can think that God can exists beyong the realms of the heavens and the earth. What does that mean?

Genesis 1:1 is important because it alluded to in John 1:1 where, "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God."

This is where it all begins.
