**<sup>6</sup>Then God said, "Let there be an expanse between the waters, separating water from water." <sup>7</sup>So God made the expanse and separated the water under the expanse from the water above the expanse. And it was so. <sup>8</sup>God called the expanse "sky." Evening came and then morning: the second day.**

God speaks for the second time. My question is what is above the "sky?" Is it still waters? Or has God done something with the waters above the sky?

Aside: one time when I was reading Genesis chapter 1, I had the realization that Creation can be an analogy to conception in a mother's womb. The formless and empty earth, the separation of the waters, and the creation of earth itself can be seen as the growth and development of a child in the womb.
