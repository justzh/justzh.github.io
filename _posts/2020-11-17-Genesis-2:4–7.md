**⁴These are the records of the heavens and the earth, concerning their creation. At the time that the LORD God made the earth and the heavens, ⁵no shrub of the field had yet grown on the land, and there was no man to work the ground. ⁶But mist would come up from the earth and water all the ground. ⁷Then the LORD God formed the man out of the dust from the ground and breathed the breath of life into his nostrils, and the man became a living being.**

The records that Moses (?) is referring to is the book of Genesis. He says that the records concern the creation of the heavens and the earth. When God made the earth and the heavens, no shrub of the field had yet grown. This means that there were no farms or fields where vegetation would grow; that is to say, agriculture had not been developed.

He also says there was no man to work the ground. So, I believe that we are in the sixth day since he had not created man yet.

What is this mist that would come up from the earth?

The man was formed from dust.

What is notable here is that there is one man, THE man who is important.

The man is a part of living beings once he has recevied the breath of life.
